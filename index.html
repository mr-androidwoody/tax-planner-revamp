IncomeFlow helps you see how your money can work through retirement, drawing from pensions, ISAs, and investments in a way that is tax-efficient and built around your life.

What it does

Year-by-year projection

Each year the engine calculates how much to draw from each account to hit your income target, accounting for growth and inflation.

England tax modelling

Income tax, CGT, dividend tax, the starting rate for savings, and Personal Savings Allowance are all calculated per person, per year.

Withdrawal strategies

Compare balanced, ISA-first, and pension-first drawdown to find which approach keeps your lifetime tax bill lowest.

Three things to have ready

  • Your accounts: what you hold, in which wrapper (SIPP, ISA, GIA, Cash), and roughly how each is invested.
  • Your income target: the gross household amount you want each year in retirement.
  • Your State Pension: your forecast age and amount, available from your Government Gateway account.

The numbers are projections, not promises. Markets move, tax rules change, and life rarely follows a straight line.

Think of IncomeFlow as a way to stress-test your plan and explore different strategies, rather than a tool that gives you one definitive answer. Start with honest assumptions, then try a few scenarios.

Presets

Select a preset to set both growth and inflation together, or enter your own values on the left. Selecting a preset updates the growth rate only - adjust inflation separately if needed.

Set growth too high and the plan may look stronger than it will be. Set it conservatively and you protect against markets underperforming. At 4% growth and 2.5% inflation, your portfolio grows at roughly 1.5% per year in real terms.

Withdrawal strategy

Each year, the projection decides which accounts to take money from: pension, ISA, or GIA. The order matters because each wrapper is taxed differently.

Pension withdrawals are partly taxable, ISA withdrawals are completely tax-free, and GIA withdrawals may trigger capital gains tax. Try each strategy and compare the lifetime tax figures in Your outlook to see which approach works best for your situation.

TaxControls how UK tax thresholds are uprated over the projection. Frozen means fiscal drag erodes real allowances each year; uprating preserves them in line with inflation.

When tax allowances don't rise with inflation, more of your income is taxed at higher rates each year. Set whether you expect allowances to stay frozen or start tracking inflation at some point.

GIA dividends

Set how dividends from your GIA holdings are handled each year.

Expected dividend yield (%/yr)Blended yield across all GIA holdings. If only part of your GIA pays dividends, weight by value. For example, £60k in a 3% dividend fund and £40k in an accumulation fund gives a blended yield of 1.8% (60% x 3%).
Treatment
Pay out counts as taxable income each year. Reinvest compounds inside the GIA and defers the tax.
Bed & ISA

Bed & ISA is a technique where you sell assets held in a General Investment Account (GIA) and immediately repurchase them inside an ISA. Once inside the ISA, all future growth, dividends, and gains are completely tax-free.

It's particularly valuable if you hold a large GIA. Each year you can shelter up to £20,000 per person from future CGT and dividend tax. The sale crystallises any gain in the year of transfer, so the engine models the CGT cost of each transfer against the long-term tax saving.

Setting up your accounts

Each row represents a single account or investment wrapper. Add one row per account — for example, a Vanguard ISA, a workplace pension, and a trading account would each be separate rows.

Wrapper
The tax wrapper this account sits in. ISAs are tax-free. SIPP/WPs are taxed as income on withdrawal (with 25% typically tax-free). GIAs are subject to dividend tax and CGT. Cash accounts earn interest within their structure.
SIPP/Workplace pension
Pension wrappers. Contributions receive tax relief. Withdrawals are typically 25% tax-free and 75% taxed as income. Access is usually restricted until pension age.
ISA (Stocks & Shares)
Fully tax-free wrapper. No tax on income or gains, and no tax on withdrawals. Annual contribution limits apply.
Cash ISA
Tax-free cash savings. Interest is not taxed. Typically used for short-term savings or cash buffers.
GIA (General Investment Account)
No tax shelter. Dividends, interest, and capital gains are taxable, with allowances available. Fully flexible access.
Cash
Standard cash holdings used for liquidity, spending, or short-term reserves rather than long-term growth.
Cashlike
Low-risk, interest-bearing assets such as money market funds. These sit between cash and bonds — typically offering modest returns with low volatility. Often used as a cash buffer within an investment portfolio.
Value (£)
The current market value of this account. This is the starting balance the engine uses — enter what it's worth right now.
Equities % / Bonds % / Cashlike %
How this account is invested. Equities drive growth, bonds provide stability, cashlike assets sit between the two. These should add up to 100% along with Cash %.
Cash %
The proportion held as uninvested cash. Cash earns no investment return — keep this low unless the account is genuinely held as cash.
Rate % AER
For interest-bearing holdings such as savings or cashlike assets. Sets the annual rate the engine uses to calculate income. Leave blank for purely invested accounts.
Draw £/mo
A fixed monthly amount drawn from this account before other wrappers are touched. Useful for modelling a regular income stream from a cash or cashlike pot. Leave blank to let the engine manage withdrawals automatically.
